## Airport Training Flights — East Venice Lead/Noise Crisis

**Status as of March 10, 2026 meeting:** Residents Matthew Marose and Bridget McBride (East Venice) made public comment for at least two consecutive meetings. A petition has 1,050 signatures. Reported 102% increase in touch-and-go training flights, translating to ~20,000 additional operations over residential neighborhoods in 2025. Santa Monica Airport is one of the highest lead emitters in LA County (leaded avgas fuel). Mayor Tarrosa expressed openness to agendizing. Staff was asked to follow up with speakers.

**Why it matters:** Environmental justice + public health + directly tied to the airport conversion timeline. East Venice residents "have no seat at the table unless you give us one."

**How to apply:** Flag any future meeting that includes airport agenda items or further public comment on this issue. Track whether it gets formally agendized.

## Airport Conversion Project — smacproject.com

**Status as of March 10, 2026:** Draft framework diagram released organizing the airport site into 8 distinct districts. Public input survey open through **April 27, 2026** at smacproject.com. City team (Peter and Amber) leading outreach to boards, commissions, and neighborhood groups. This project was initiated under Public Works Director Rick Falte (retired March 2026); successor is Chris Bischlip.

**Why it matters:** One of the most consequential land use decisions in Santa Monica's future. Will shape housing, parks, mobility, and environmental outcomes for decades.

**How to apply:** Flag the April 27 survey deadline in any meeting analysis before that date. Track future master plan hearings.

## FPPC Conflict of Interest — Mayor Pro Tem Jesse Zwick / HAC

**Status as of March 10, 2026:** California FPPC issued a formal ruling that Zwick has a disqualifying conflict of interest on housing votes due to his employment as Southern California Director of the Housing Action Coalition (HAC). HAC's CEO offered Zwick the job three days after Zwick voted on a developer-friendly affordable housing ordinance (August 2025). City has been forced to initiate ratification proceedings on multiple housing-related actions (duplex regs, small lot subdivisions, building standard updates). Zwick was absent from March 10 meeting.

**Why it matters:** Creates policy uncertainty on housing votes; pro-housing decisions may need to be re-taken. Direct relevance to SMF housing priority.

**How to apply:** In future meeting analyses, flag any ratification votes or housing votes where Zwick's participation (or absence) is relevant.

## ADU/Addition Conflict — 1213 Pine Street

**Status as of March 10, 2026:** Property owner Fred Larian and attorney Jackson McNeill (Elkins Kalt) made public comment seeking either permit approval or formal agendization. Staff's position: the ADU law's intent was to add units, not enable later removal. Result: homeowner must demolish and rebuild the ADU to get an addition permit — an outcome both parties describe as absurd. Council asked staff to follow up; not agendized.

**Why it matters:** Could set precedent affecting all ADU owners who want to make additions. May create chilling effect on future ADU construction.

**How to apply:** If a future agenda item addresses ADU policy or R1 zoning, note connection to this case.

## Ocean Avenue Project — Transparency Dispute

**Status as of March 10, 2026:** Ongoing. Public commenter Mike Montgomery (at least 2nd appearance on this topic) alleges Mayor Tarrosa misrepresented her prior knowledge of an Ocean Avenue supportive housing project for people with severe mental illness at the October 14, 2025 council meeting. Emails reportedly contradict her "point of personal privilege" claim. Tarrosa did not respond publicly at the March 10 meeting.

**Why it matters:** Civic health and transparency concern. Also touches on community notification for supportive housing siting.

**How to apply:** If Mayor Tarrosa takes any action to address this, or if it escalates, note it.
