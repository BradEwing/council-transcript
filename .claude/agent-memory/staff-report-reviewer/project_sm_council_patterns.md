Observed procedural patterns from March 10, 2026 meeting:

- **Consent calendar procedure:** Clerk reads items, staff or council members can pull items. Pulled items voted separately after the bulk consent vote. Ordinances read by title only unless a member objects.
- **Levine Act disclosures:** Asked at start of meeting; council members self-report conflicts.
- **Conflict recusal pattern:** Council member leaves dais and is marked absent for that vote (e.g., Hall recused from 4A).
- **Commendations:** Read by the Mayor, followed by staff/City Manager remarks, then the honoree speaks. Not voted on.
- **Closed session:** Held after consent calendar. Items include litigation (existing and potential), real estate negotiations, and public employee appointments. Report-outs happen in open session after closed session.
- **City Manager report:** Includes community events, project milestones, and "adopted pet of the week."
- **Council composition (as of March 2026):** Mayor Trosses, Mayor Pro Tem Swick (Jesse), Councilmembers Hall, Raskin, Snow, Zarnetskaya, Negrette. City Manager: Oliver Chi.
- **Public Works Director succession:** Rick Falte retired March 2026; Chris Bischlip is successor.

**Why:** Understanding these patterns helps structure future meeting analyses and anticipate where council may deviate from staff recommendations.
**How to apply:** Use these patterns to organize analysis reports and flag deviations from normal procedure.
